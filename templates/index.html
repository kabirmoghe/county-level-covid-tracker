Learn about the spread of COVID-19 in your county

This web app allows you to explore the current month's COVID-19 infection rate across 90% of counties in the United States. The data used is compiled from USAFacts.org (the source for the CDC's COVID-19 statistics), USDA, and AARP. The data on the COVID-19 pandemic includes monthly information on cases and deaths as well as infection rate and mortality rate, though infection rate is primarily used as a measure of severity of spread in a county. The app incorporates a 3-day lag in the used COVID-19 data in order to prevent errors in the data sources from affecting the calculated rates, though these errors are usually fixed within days. Along with information on COVID-19, the app includes several other county attributes that may be relevant to the spread of the virus: median household income, unemployment, education, race demographics, and the presence of statewide mask requirements. All of these attributes are up-to-date, and the mask-requirements are updated almost daily.

To find information on a county, go to the Data tab and enter your county (in the form "Your County Name", "Capitalized State Abbreviation") and select the correct county.

Note: the first letters of your county name should be capitalized.
